About Us

The McKnight Brain Research Foundation, established in 1999 by Evelyn F. McKnight, is dedicated to enhancing our understanding and treatment of age-related cognitive decline and memory loss. It is the only private foundation solely focused on unraveling the mysteries of the aging brain and promoting cognitive health throughout an individual’s lifetime. With an investment exceeding $180 million, the foundation has been pivotal in funding groundbreaking research and fostering initiatives through its partnerships with the four McKnight Brain Institutes and the National Institute on Aging.

The foundation's strategic pillars emphasize leadership in establishing dedicated research on cognitive decline, inspiring public knowledge and maintaining cognitive health, and fostering strong partnerships with key scientific and academic bodies. These collaborations have led to significant advancements, including the establishment of endowed chairs in honor of Evelyn McKnight at various institutes, aimed at driving research in neurology and the neurobiology of learning and memory.

The McKnight Brain Institutes, integral components of the foundation’s efforts, are hubs of multidisciplinary research aimed at understanding cognitive aging and developing interventions to maintain and enhance cognitive function. These institutes, located at the universities of Alabama, Arizona, Florida, and Miami, bring together leading researchers to explore and address the neurobiological bases of memory and aging, supported by cutting-edge facilities and collaborative networks that enrich the field of cognitive neuroscience.
