About

Researchers are improving methods to predict brain age from Magnetic Resonance Images (MRIs) through machine learning. These models calculate the difference between predicted brain age and actual age (brain-PAD), serving as an indicator of either pathology or healthy aging. One challenge is that most models are developed using high-quality research MRIs, which differ significantly from routine clinical scans in terms of variability and resolution. To address this issue, initiatives are focusing on enhancing data collection from diverse MRI modalities, including routine clinical scans. Techniques like Synthetic Super-Resolution are employed to upgrade the resolution of these clinical images to match research-grade data. This refined data collection strategy helps in adapting the models to be effective across various MRI settings, making brain age prediction a versatile tool for both research and clinical diagnostics. Improved data collection supports the broader application of brain age as a diagnostic measure, facilitating personalized medicine and proactive disease management.
