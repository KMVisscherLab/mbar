Resources

This page serves as a central hub for accessing a variety of resources offered by the McKnight Brain Aging Registry (MBAR). Researchers and collaborators can find an array of tools, publications, comprehensive datasets, and detailed guidelines, all meticulously curated to facilitate and advance the study of brain aging and cognitive decline. These resources are instrumental for conducting analyses, comparing methodologies, and understanding the broader implications of our findings. We encourage you to utilize these materials to deepen your research and expand the scope of knowledge in the field of cognitive aging.

Mission
  • Data Harmonization and Quality Control
  • Collaborative Data Sharing
  • Ongoing Data Acquisition

The mission of the McKnight Brain Aging Registry (MBAR) is to enhance the understanding of brain aging and cognitive decline through a collaborative, multidisciplinary approach. By harmonizing and rigorously quality-checking neuropsychological, behavioral, biological, and neuroimaging data across the Evelyn F. McKnight Brain Institute sites, MBAR aims to build a comprehensive, high-quality dataset. This dataset is not only used for robust cross-site comparisons and pioneering research but is also shared with the broader scientific community to foster collaborative advancements. Our commitment extends to continuous data acquisition and the promotion of global research efforts into age-related cognitive disorders, paving the way for improved diagnostics, preventive strategies, and treatments.
